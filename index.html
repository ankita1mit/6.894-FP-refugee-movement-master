Events Sparking Refugee Movements

Refugee movements can occur due to a wide variety of inciting events. Now, we'll explore three specific major refugee movements due to: Genocide, Politics, and Civil War.

Rwanda


1994

Ethnic Genocide

In 1994, existing ethnic tensions in Rwanda led to the start of a genocidal campaign that would ultimately claim of the lives of an estimated 1 million Rwandan citizens.

Prior German and Belgium colonial administrations had created artificial rifts between the Hutu, Tutsi and Twa ethnic groups in Rwanda. These groups were alternatively disenfranchised or empowered in pursuit of the colonial goals and ethnic divisions were reinforced via government and administrative policies.
Violence between the Hutu and Tutsi groups occurred spontaneously eventually leading to an ongoing civil war between the Hutu government and the Tutsi Rwandan Patriotic Front rebel group. The general Rwandan civilian population, however, was still comprised of all three ethnic groups.
The civil war underwent a cease-fire in 1993 and the Rwandan dictator Juvenal Habyarimana had agreed to a new transitional government. However, Habyarimana’s assassination in 1994 led to the start of a genoicidal campaign by extremist Hutus against Rwanda’s Tutsi, Twa and moderate Hutu population.

The genocide was perpetrated by extremist Hutu groups against Rwanda's Tutsi, Twa, and moderate Hutu populations.

Extremist Hutus

The attacks were carried out by various groups with anti-Tutsi sentiment and those supporting Hutu Power. These included the Hutu government and military, the Interahamwe and Impuzamugambi Hutu militia groups as well as sympathetic Hutu civilians that joined in on the killings.

Rwandan Civilians

The genoicidal campaign targeted a wide swath of groups that were seen as enemies to the Hutu Power movement. This started with key Tutsi and moderate Hutus in the military or political spheres, however the killings were coordinated to target Rwandan civilians as well.

The Rwandan Genocide led to generalized chaos throughout the entire region, starting a mass Refugee exodus.

  • The killings quickly spread throughout the entire country as the organized extremist Hutus successfully persuaded Hutu civilians to participate in the genocide.

  • This extreme action quickly restarted the Rwandan Civil War and the rebel RFP sprang into action to retake the country, adding to the chaos.

  • The indiscriminate killing led to a mass exodus of non-Hutu civilians, desperate to flee the genocide

The eventual outcomes of the Rwandan Genocide were:

In the end, an estimated 1 Million Rwandans lost their lives in the genocide, including 70% of the country's Tutsi population.
The destruction caused by the genocide and civil war decimated the country's infrastrcuture, economy and governmental institutions, as key people were either murdered or fled the country.
The renewed civil war led to the downfall of the extremist administration, as the RFP took power. This led to another exodus, this time of Hutus, fearing reprisals.
In the end, 2 million Rwandans fled the country as refugees, fleeing largely to neighboring countries in Africa.

Venezuela


1997 - Present

Politics

The ongoing Bolivarian Revolution of the Hugo Chavez and Nicolas Maduro administrations has created a socioeconomic crisis in Venezuela.

The Bolvarian revolution started with the administration of Hugo Chavez in 1998, who started Bolivarian missions to provide public services. Many of these policies did in fact raise quality of life for the lower class population in Venezuela.
However these populist policies began to yield economic difficulties for the country as they proved unsustainable. Nevertheless, Nicolas Maduro, who succeeded Chavez in 2013, doubled down on these socialist policies which created high inflation rates and goods shortage
These policies continued the Venezuelan decline, creating a general deterioration of both the economic and social situation in the country. These conditions have led to a mass emigration from Venezuela, resulting in what is termed the Bolivarian diaspora, throughout South America.

The Bolivarian Revolution is mainly supported by the Maduro administration and inflicted upon the general Venezuelan population.

Bolivarian Revolution Supporters

The main "aggressors" in this conflict are the Chavez and Maduro administrations as well as the goverment groups at their disposal. The party currently in control is the Simon Bolivar Great Patriotic Pole, a left-wing socialist group that supports the Bolivarian Revolution and blames problems on the "economic war" beign waged on Venezuela.

Bolivarian Revolution Victims

The victims of this Bolivarian Revolution are the general civilian population that is subject to the deteriorating economic, political and social conditions in the country. The main political opposition group in Venezuela is the Democratic Unity Roundtable, a center to center-left political group that opposes the Chavist and Madurist economic policies.

The ongoing Bolivarian Revolution has led to a general deterioration to the quality of life of the Venezuelan population.

  • The policies of the Chavez and Maduro administrations led to one of the most severe economic crises facing a country in recent history. These economic policies have resulted in runaway, rampant hyperinflation as well as goods shortages that have increased the poverty level in Venezuela. Many citizens are unable to access basic needs and goods required for survival, such as food.

  • The unfavorable economic state has also led to a deterioration in social conditions as many Venezuelans are in a state of poverty. This has resulted in an extraordinary growth in the crime and murder rate in the country. Additionally many citizens lack access to basic health services as poverty levels increased resulting in a dire healthcare condition

  • The instability has also resulted in reprisals and political repression by the Maduro administration upon protesting citizens. There are reports of politically motivated arrests and disappearances as well as human rights violations of detainees.

The result has been a mass emigration from Venezuela so large that the group of refugees has been termed the Bolivarian Diaspora.

The emigration from Venezuela started gradually with the Chavez administration in 1998. The initial emigrants were primarily the wealthy citizens of Venezuela, anticipating oncoming difficulties with the socialistic policies.
The emigration greatly accelerated in 2015, as Maduro doubled-down on the socialistic policies and the general condition of Venezueal deteriorated. These emigrants were comprised primarily of young professionals seeking improved quality of life in other countries.
As of today, 4.5 million Venezuelan citizens have fled the socio-economic insecurity of the country and sought refuge in other countries.

Syria


2011-Present

Civil War

2011 marks the start of the Syrian Civil war, an ongoing conflict and humanitarian crisis that continues to this day

In the early 2010s, the Middle-Eastern world saw a series of anti-government protests and uprisings that became known as the Arab Spring. The protests opposed the oppressive regimes and low quality of life in severl countries throughout the region, including Syria.
Major protests and unrest occurred in Damascus and Aleppo, opposing the current al-Assad regime. The Assad regime began a series of brutal crackdowns resulting in thousands of civilian deaths and the start of an armed insurgency opposing the government.
Since then the conflict has developed into an ongoing quagmire that has grown to include numerous internal and external groups as combatants. The dangers posed to Syrian civilians by this conflict have resulted in one of the largest humanitarian emergencies in recent history and leading millions to flee the country.

The Syrian Conflict is composed of a complex shifting patchwork of Combatant Groups, creating widespread instability and chaos throughout the region

Pro-Assad Groups

The government is supported by numerous internal groups such as the Syrian Armed Forces and National Defens group. There are also numerous unofficial militia groups, such as the Shabiha, that support the current government in its campaigns. External groups also support the current Assad regime, these involve state actors such as Hezbollah as well as nations such as Iran and Russia.

Syrian Opposition

The anti-Assad effort is mainly headed up by the Free Syrian Army, made up by defectors from the Syrian Army. The effort is also organized by the National Coaliting an interim government intending to take power. However the opposition to the government is not unified, there are other groups such as the Syrian Salvation Govermnet and National Coordination Committee with different goals leading to infighting. Finally there is also the involvement of jihadi groups, taking advantage of the current instability to take power.

The chaos and dangers posed by the multiple armed groups operating in the region have destroyed the once peaceful fabric of Syrian society.

  • What started as an internal civil war, led to the intervention of numerous groups as a result civilian populations faced threats from multiple combatants.

  • The Syrian population faced human rights abuses from numerous angles, from the government which deployed chemical weapons non-speifically, to extremist Islamist groups that murdered civilians en masse.

  • This led to the intervention of outside groups such as Russia, Turkey, the Kurds and the United States. These groups unfortunately supported seperate factions intensifying the combat and further adding to the instability.

  • Multiple cease-fire and buffer zone agreements were attempted but quickly fell apart as the conflict raged on, forcing civilians to either flee or continue to live amongst the fighting.

The Syrian conflict represents one of the largest humanitarian crises in recent history, one that continues to affect Syrian citizens to this day

  • The conflict has resulted in an estimated 118,000 civilian deaths for a total of 586,100 deaths.

  • Over 7.6 million Syrians are internally displaced and 5.1 million are currently refugees

  • The conflict is currently ongoing, putting more civilians in hardship and complicating efforts to resettle the refugees.

The Human Experience


These individuals face incomparable struggles, not only in fleeing the situations in their home country, but also in adapting to their new environment.

Monica

Hutu Survivor of the Rwandan Genocide


This Monica, a Hutu survivor of the Rwandan genocide. Due to her marriage to a Tutsi she was targeted by her own family, her Hutu brothers and father. Her six children and husband were slaughtered in front of her eyes. She narrowly escaped the violence herself fleeing from her own family. In order to flee the ongoing violence, Monica fled to Tanzania where she lived in a refugee camp. Since the time of the genocide she has repatriated to Rwanda. She draws support from a dialogue club which seeks to counsel survivors and bring together both Hutu and Tutsi to create a dialogue and community


Cibel Ortiz

Venezuelan Refugee living in Colombia


This is Cibel Ortiz, a Venezuelan citizen who left the country to come into Colombia as a refugee. Cibel fled Venezuela on foot, walking for seven days with her husband and 2-month old baby in order to reach Colombia. In Venezuela, Cibel had lost her business which sold cleaning supplies as the economy collapsed. She recalls rampant food insecurity with people scavenging for food in the garbage. She lacked the essentials such as milk and diapers for her newborn child, this lack of necessities precipitated the difficult decision to abandon Venzuela and leaving other family members behind.


However, life still proved difficult once they reached Colombia. After a week of living on the streets the family found themselves in a barebones apartment. Cibel supported her family by selling snacks to bus passengers for 12 hours a day. However, this was barely enough to make ends meet and her son Matias often went hungry. To this day she struggles with making ends meet and relies on the assistance of charitable organizations.

The Kalaji Family

Syrian Refugee Family living in Philadelphia


This is the Kalaji family, a family of Syrian citizens that was able to escape the violence of the Syrian Civil war. Originally from Aleppo, the family was able to escape to Joran which served as a temporary home for the group. Eventually the family was able to seek a new life in the Philadelphia, one of the last Syrian refugees to be able to enter the US before the Travel Ban. However, this difficult decision resulted in them leaving behind their eldest pregnant daughter and other family members.


The family still faced difficulties once in Philadelphia in terms of integrating into American society but also in terms of financial self-sufficiency. The refugee aid organization was only able to financially support the family for 3 months. The family is currently living in Philadelphia, continuing to adjust to their new life as refugees in the US.
